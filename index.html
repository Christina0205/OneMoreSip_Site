01

The Game

Inspiration · Concept · Gameplay footage

Inspiration

The game grew out of a familiar East-Asian campus feeling: the dorm is never fully private. Even after class, you are still inside a system — corridors are watched, guards patrol, teachers appear without warning. One More Sip takes that low-grade surveillance and pushes it into dark comedy.

It borrows from two unlikely places at once: the patrol-and-hide tension of stealth games, and the deliberately awkward controls of physics-comedy games. A spy thriller's machinery, scaled down to a student hiding a bottle behind his back.

Concept

Two levels, two kinds of danger. In the corridor, the threat is external — authority figures who must not catch you drinking. In the bathroom, the threat becomes internal — your own drunk body, refusing to aim straight.

Every reward carries a cost. Drinking scores points, but each bottle makes the world spin, drift, and reverse. The player physically feels the student losing control. Relief and punishment are the same action.

Level 1 corridor stealth → Level 2 drunken bathroom aiming.

Designs

Life experience · Core emotion · Core mechanics

Life experience

Student exhaustion after a long academic day. The dorm as shelter and institution. The small, hidden rebellion of unwinding where you're not supposed to.

Core emotion

The tense triangle of relaxation, guilt, and punishment. Wanting to escape pressure, and watching that escape become embarrassing and uncontrollable.

Core mechanic

Risk–reward. Drinking gives points but raises difficulty. The more you score, the harder it gets to score again. The system punishes its own incentive.

Difficulty vs. Drunk Level

Two curves cross: as the score climbs, control falls. The gap between them is where the comedy — and the failure — lives.

03

Game World

Visual & audio · Space · Cultural layers · Narrative

Space 01

The Corridor

Transitional space between public control and private rest. Close to the dorm, still fully visible.

  • Long horizontal hallway, narrow path
  • Dorm doors · blue-gray tiled walls
  • Dim yellow lights, scattered bottles
  • Teachers & guards on patrol routes

Tense, repetitive — discipline, surveillance, the fear of being caught.

Space 02

The Bathroom

Enclosed and small. The danger is no longer the guards — it's the student's own unstable body.

  • Tiled wall and floor · cold lighting
  • Urinal · round trash can · framed picture
  • Flickering light, empty surrounding space
  • An ordinary room made strange by drunkenness

An absurd performance stage where wrong targets become "valuable."

Visual & audio identity

A muted dormitory palette — dark blue-gray, dirty beige, dull yellow lamplight, cold tile. Realistic, tired, slightly depressing. As the student gets drunk the image destabilizes: rotation, blur, double vision, camera shake, color distortion.

Audio is mostly ambient music: calm in Level 1, light and humorous in Level 2, slightly distorted at high drunkenness. The bathroom adds a comedic twist — each target plays a different note:

Urinal Do +2 Trash can Re +1 Picture Mi +3 Floor — −1/0

Cultural layers

The game is rooted in East-Asian dormitory life — school discipline and surveillance, student stress culture, the late-night campus atmosphere, and the quiet rule-breaking that happens in shared corridors and bathrooms.

Its comedy is built on embarrassment and loss of control. The corridor, bathroom, teacher, guard, and dorm room aren't just scenery — together they form one controlled system the student can never quite step outside of.

Narrative design — story told through space

A long day

A tired student heads back to the dorm under pressure.

The shortcut

He drinks in secret to relax — and must hide it from authority.

Escalation

Each reward deepens the consequence. The corridor tightens.

Collapse

In the bathroom, body, vision, sound and controls all give way.

The corridor is social control; the bathroom is personal loss of control. Two rooms, one comic-but-uncomfortable story about pressure, hidden rebellion, and the failure of escape.
